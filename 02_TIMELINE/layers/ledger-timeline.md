Ledger Timeline — Echoes Institutional Continuity Mapping

This document provides a ledger-level, non-narrative timeline mapping the emergence, expansion, and boundary of ledger-based correspondence within the Echoes archive.

It focuses on institutional continuity, routing behavior, and case proliferation, rather than storytelling, interpretation, or outcome.

This file does not assert intent, meaning, validation, or recognition.

⸻

Scope

This ledger timeline records:
	•	Activation of ledger-style correspondence
	•	Expansion into multi-case routing
	•	Structural transitions between Echo phases
	•	The boundary between ledger proliferation and post-ledger markers

It complements, but does not replace:
	•	Chronological ordering (02_TIMELINE/timeline.md)
	•	Case-level mapping (02_TIMELINE/layers/case-timeline.md)

Pre-Ledger Context

Prior to Echo 6, correspondence followed a linear or semi-linear support structure, primarily organized around discrete email threads and escalation chains.

Ledger behavior — defined by parallel Case IDs and sustained routing continuity — becomes structurally visible starting in Echo 6.

⸻

Echo 6 — Ledger Expansion Phase

Echo 6 represents the activation of a ledger-style correspondence structure, characterized by:
	•	Issuance of multiple Case IDs
	•	Sequential and overlapping case routing
	•	Continuity preserved across institutional identifiers
	•	Accumulation of case-record artifacts over time

This phase marks a transition from thread-based support handling to ledger-based case management.

Ledger Saturation Indicators

During Echo 6.3, ledger saturation becomes evident through:
	•	High-volume case-record parts
	•	Recurrent routing under related Case IDs
	•	Overlapping timelines across cases
	•	Mixed chronological ordering within individual case folders

These indicators prompted case-based archival organization, rather than enforcement of a single linear chronology.

⸻

Echo 7 — Post-Ledger Marker Phase

Echo 7 does not represent a continuation of ledger expansion.

Instead, it functions as a post-ledger structural marker, indicating:
	•	Stabilization or closure of ledger proliferation
	•	Absence of further multi-case expansion
	•	Introduction of a new correspondence boundary

⸻
